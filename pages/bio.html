About Me

I am a Ph.D. student in Bioinformatics and Integrative Genomics (BIG) in the Department of Biomedical Informatics of Harvard Medical School. I graduated from the University of California, Berkeley with a B.S. in bioengineering in 2015. Before returning to school, I worked as an ORISE research fellow at the Food and Drug Administration and as a research specialist in the H. Benjamin Larman Lab of the Department of Pathology at Johns Hopkins University School of Medicine. In the Larman Lab, I performed high-throughput, massively-multiplexed immune profiling assays and designed software for related data analyses.
